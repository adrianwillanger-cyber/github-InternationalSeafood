International Crab Market Report – May 2026

Subtitle: Global crab pricing, supply conditions, and export flows across North America, Russia, and Asia.
The global crab market entered May with tight supply conditions and steady demand across Asia and North America. Russian supply remains constrained by quota pacing and logistical bottlenecks, while North American producers continue to face mixed harvest conditions. Asian buyers remain selective but active, with premium grades maintaining strong pricing.

Export Flow Summary

North American exports softened slightly due to variable harvests and weather‑related delays. Russian shipments remained below historical averages, supporting firm pricing in key Asian markets. China and South Korea maintained steady import activity, while Japan increased purchases of premium grades to replenish inventories.

Pricing Trends

Pricing remained firm across most crab categories. Snow crab prices held steady due to limited supply, while king crab pricing showed mild upward pressure. Blue crab pricing was stable, supported by consistent retail demand. Freight costs remain a secondary factor but have not significantly impacted landed prices.

Species Notes

• Snow Crab: Limited supply; firm pricing.
• King Crab: Mild upward pressure; premium grades strong.
• Blue Crab: Stable demand; consistent retail movement.
• Dungeness: Regional variability; pricing supported by tight supply.

Risks and Opportunities

Risks include geopolitical uncertainty affecting Russian supply, variable North American harvest conditions, and potential freight volatility. Opportunities include strong Asian demand for premium grades and stable retail interest in mid‑priced crab categories.

Summary

The international crab market remains supply‑tight with firm pricing. Asian demand continues to support premium categories, while North American and Russian supply constraints maintain upward pressure on global prices.
