Japan Seafood Market Report – May 2026

Import flows, wholesale pricing, and species‑specific demand patterns across crab, salmon, and high‑value shellfish.

Market Overview

Japan’s seafood market entered late spring with steady import activity and cautious wholesale buying. Retail demand remains consistent, while foodservice continues to recover. The yen’s relative stability has reduced pricing volatility, supporting predictable import flows across crab, salmon, and shellfish categories.

Import Flows

Crab imports from Canada and the U.S. increased slightly as inventories were replenished. Salmon imports from Chile and Norway remained stable, with Japan maintaining its position as a premium buyer. Shellfish imports, particularly scallops and clams, saw modest growth driven by retail promotions and steady household consumption.

Pricing Trends

Crab pricing held firm, supported by limited global supply. Salmon pricing softened slightly as global inventories normalized. Shellfish pricing remained stable, with minor upward pressure from freight and cold‑storage costs.

Species Notes

• Crab: Strong demand for premium grades; stable wholesale pricing.
• Salmon: Chilean and Norwegian supply consistent; slight price easing.
• Shellfish: Scallops and clams steady; retail demand healthy.
• Tuna: Fresh tuna pricing elevated due to seasonal supply constraints.

Risks & Opportunities

Risks include potential yen weakening, freight cost increases, and global supply tightness in crab. Opportunities include stable retail demand, improved foodservice recovery, and Japan’s continued preference for premium imported species.

Summary

Japan’s seafood market remains balanced with predictable import flows and stable pricing. Crab demand is strong, salmon is steady, and shellfish continues to perform well. Market conditions are favorable heading into early summer.
